About Me

I am a third-year PhD student and Graduate Student Researcher in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley, advised by Dr. Tina Chow. My research focuses on atmospheric science, combining high-resolution WRF-Chem large-eddy simulations with Python- and GIS-based analysis to study landfill methane dispersion and evaluate methane observations from stationary, mobile, and remote-sensing platforms.

Prior to UC Berkeley, I completed an MSc at the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth, focusing on superhydrophobic surfaces, antibiofouling behavior, and 3D bacterial tracking using digital holography. I earned my BS from Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology (BUET), with a focus on computational fluid dynamics and high-speed aerodynamics, and I aim to become a leading expert in interdisciplinary fluid-related research.
