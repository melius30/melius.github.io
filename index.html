About Me

I am a third-year PhD student in Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley, specializing in atmospheric modeling. My doctoral research focuses on methane emissions from municipal solid waste landfills, where I combine advanced modeling techniques with measurement strategies to reduce uncertainties and support effective mitigation. As part of an EPA-funded project, I use the WRF-Chem model to evaluate methane emission dynamics, optimize sensor network design, and assess climate impacts, with the goal of providing scientific insight to inform sustainable waste management and climate policy. Prior to pursuing my PhD, I earned a master’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth in 2023. My master’s research centered on marine biofouling, where I investigated the performance and durability of superhydrophobic surfaces in marine environments. I completed my bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) in 2019, with a research focus on computational fluid mechanics, particularly supersonic jet flows. From my early academic training to my current doctoral work, my research path reflects a growing commitment to applying engineering tools to address pressing environmental challenges and to advancing sustainable climate solutions.

Research Interests

  • Atmospheric Modeling: Large-Eddy Simulations (LES), WRF-Chem, turbulence-resolving plume dynamics
  • Methane Emissions: Landfill CH₄ dispersion, source characterization, facility-scale modeling
  • Model–Observation Integration: Statistical comparison with stationary, mobile, and remote-sensing measurements
  • Data Analysis & Geospatial Tools: Python (Xarray, SciPy), GIS, raster-based analysis
  • Fluid Mechanics: Experimental and computational fluid dynamics, atmospheric flows
